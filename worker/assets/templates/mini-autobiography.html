The arc

How I got here

2014 · The first build

A weekend that rewired me

I rebuilt my high-school newspaper's website over a single weekend because the old one embarrassed me. It was ugly, it was hand-coded, and it worked. I was hooked on the feeling of shipping something real.

2017 · The pivot

I left the program to learn for real

Dropped out of the design degree to take a support job at a four-person startup. I learned more in six months of answering angry tickets and fixing the product overnight than two years of critiques ever taught me.

2020 · The craft

Led a redesign 200,000 people use daily

Owned the rebuild of a product's core flow end to end — research, design, and the front-end code. Churn dropped, activation climbed, and I learned that taste without follow-through is just an opinion.

Now · Independent

Calm, fast, dependency-free

I build for founders who need it to just work — no fragile toolchains, no 2 a.m. outages from a library that vanished. Things that load instantly and last for years.
